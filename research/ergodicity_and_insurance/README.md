# Ergodicity and Insurance

Explorations on ergodicity and random processes, based on the work of Ole Peters and Alexander Adamou.

A positive-EV multiplicative gamble (heads +60%, tails -50%) drives individual wealth to zero over time. The system is **non-ergodic**: the ensemble average diverges from the time average. This insight resolves Kenneth Arrow's puzzle of why insurance exists — under the time-average growth rate paradigm, both insurer and merchant benefit from the contract.

## References

- [Insurance makes wealth grow faster](https://arxiv.org/abs/1507.04655) — Peters & Adamou
- [Ergodicity Economics lecture notes](https://ergodicityeconomics.com/lecture-notes/) — Peters
